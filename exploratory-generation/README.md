# Exploratory Generation

This folder contains the exploratory mnemonic-generation materials produced during the development phase of the project.

Before the final controlled experiment was narrowed to Polish, the project explored whether AI-generated visual keyword mnemonics could be produced reliably across multiple languages. This exploratory stage was used to compare the quality, clarity, and educational usefulness of generated mnemonic materials before deciding which language would be most suitable for the final user study.

## Purpose of this folder

The materials in this folder are included as research artefacts because they document the earlier generation phase that informed the final design decisions of the project.

In particular, this exploratory work helped answer questions such as:

- which languages were most suitable for AI-generated visual keyword mnemonics
- what kinds of sound-based cues were easy or difficult for the model to generate
- what recurring failure modes appeared in the outputs
- why Polish was chosen for the final controlled study

## Important note on model choice

All materials in this folder were generated using **ChatGPT**.

Gemini was tested briefly during the early exploratory stage, especially on Polish examples, but it was abandoned early because its outputs were often more sentence-like than mnemonic, less visually distinctive, and less useful for this project. For that reason, the exploratory artefacts preserved here are ChatGPT-based outputs rather than a full cross-model archive.

## Subfolders

### `polish/`
Contains exploratory Polish items that were generated during the broader development phase but were **not selected** for the final 10-word controlled study.

### `spanish/`
Contains exploratory Spanish mnemonic materials generated during multilingual testing. These were useful for comparison, but Spanish was not used in the final controlled experiment.

### `mandarin/`
Contains a small number of exploratory Mandarin examples. Generation in Mandarin was not continued because the outputs were less suitable for this mnemonic approach and exposed important limitations of the pipeline.

## Relationship to the final experiment

The materials in this folder are **exploratory only**.

They were not part of the final scored Polish experiment. Their value lies in showing the development path of the project and supporting the narrowing of scope to a more controlled final study.
