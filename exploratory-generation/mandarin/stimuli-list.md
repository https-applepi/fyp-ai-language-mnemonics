# Stimuli List

This file lists the Mandarin vocabulary items explored for mnemonic generation and briefly describes the mnemonic logic behind each stimulus.

## 1. yuè (月)
- **English meaning:** moon
- **Mnemonic idea:** The image links *yuè* to “yeah!” by showing a moon saying “yeah!” This is weak because the pinyin *yuè* does not appear in the image, so the learner is left with an English exclamation rather than a clear pronunciation cue.
- **Image file:** `yue.png`

## 2. shān (山)
- **English meaning:** mountain
- **Mnemonic idea:** The image links *shān* to “shiny” by showing a shiny mountain. This is weak because *shiny* does not sound especially close to *shān*, and the pinyin is not shown, so the learner may only remember the meaning *mountain* without learning how to say it.
- **Image file:** `shan.png`

## 3. huǒ (火)
- **English meaning:** fire
- **Mnemonic idea:** The image links *huǒ* to “whoa!” by showing a shocked person reacting to fire. This is weak because the sound cue is approximate, the pinyin is missing, and the learner may remember the reaction more strongly than the pronunciation *huǒ*.
- **Image file:** `huo.png`

## 4. shuǐ (水)
- **English meaning:** water
- **Mnemonic idea:** The image links *shuǐ* to “sway” by showing water swaying. This is weak because motion is difficult to communicate clearly in a still image, and without the pinyin being present the learner may not make a strong connection to the pronunciation *shuǐ*.
- **Image file:** `shui.png`
