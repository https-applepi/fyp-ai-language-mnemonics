# Exploratory Polish Materials

This folder contains exploratory Polish mnemonic items generated during the broader development phase of the project.

These items were produced before the final 10-word experimental set was fixed. They are included here to document the selection process and to show that not all generated Polish mnemonics were equally suitable for beginner vocabulary learning.

## Why these items were not chosen

The words and mnemonics in this folder were not selected for the final controlled study for several reasons:

- some relied on **inappropriate or undesirable references**, such as alcohol-related cues
- some depended on **very specific cultural or dialectal references** that were not ideal for broad beginner use
- some had **weak or unnatural sound-links**
- some produced mnemonics that were memorable in an unhelpful way
- some were simply less clear, less interpretable, or less educationally useful than the final chosen set

## Examples of exclusion reasons

Examples of issues that led to exclusion included:

- **alcohol-related cueing**, such as linking *woda* to *vodka*, which was considered inappropriate for the intended beginner-learning setting
- **overly specific English references**, such as linking *las* to *lass*, which depends on a specific Scottish English usage and was therefore less globally accessible
- **poor sound-link quality**, where the cue did not provide a strong or natural pronunciation bridge for an English-speaking beginner
- **overall weak mnemonic design**, where the cue and meaning were not integrated clearly enough to make a strong learning aid

## Why this folder matters

These materials are useful because they show that the final Polish stimulus set was not chosen arbitrarily. The final 10 words used in the experiment were selected because they were more concrete, more beginner-friendly, and better suited to visually clear and educationally useful mnemonic treatment than many of the other originally generated options.

## Model note

All items in this folder were generated with **ChatGPT** during the exploratory generation phase.
