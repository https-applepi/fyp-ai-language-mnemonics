# Exploratory Spanish Materials

This folder contains the exploratory Spanish mnemonic materials generated during the multilingual development phase of the project.

Spanish was used as one of the comparison languages during early testing. The purpose was to see whether AI-generated visual keyword mnemonics would work similarly across different language families, rather than only in one specific case.

## What is included here

This folder contains exploratory Spanish outputs generated by ChatGPT. These materials were not used in the final controlled experiment, but they are included as research artefacts because they helped inform the language-selection process.

## Why Spanish was not used in the final experiment

Spanish often produced plausible mnemonic outputs, but the materials were generally less distinctive and less informative for the final controlled evaluation than the Polish set.

Examples of issues that led to exclusion included:

- **using the actual definition as the mnenomic**, such as linking *rosa* with *rose* or *fruta* with *fruit*. This seemed to be due to the similarity between English and Spanish which led to the AI choosing easily linked words but defeats the purpose of keyword mnemonic learning.
- **overall weak mnemonic design and unhelpful imagery**, such as linking *sol* with *soul*, linking *agua* with *aqua* and simply labelling the water with aqua.

A further practical reason was the risk of prior participant familiarity. Spanish is commonly encountered and often taught in schools, which would have increased the chance that some participants already knew part of the vocabulary. That would have made the final experiment harder to interpret cleanly.

For these reasons, Spanish remained part of the exploratory phase but was not taken forward into the final controlled study.

## Why this folder matters

This folder is included to document that the project did not begin with Polish alone. Instead, the final scope emerged through exploration, comparison, and filtering. The Spanish materials therefore help show the broader research path and support the final methodological decisions.
