# Group Allocation

## Overview

The final experiment used a six-group counterbalanced design. Every participant studied the same 10 Polish words, but the condition assigned to each word depended on the group.

The three conditions were:

- **Image + explanation**
- **Explanation only**
- **Word only**

## Final group design

| Group | Participant IDs | Image + explanation | Explanation only | Word only |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 | dom, kot, okno | ptak, ser, zima | sklep, stary, torba, pies |
| 2 | 7, 9, 8, 10, 11 | sklep, stary, torba | dom, kot, okno, ser | pies, ptak, zima |
| 3 | 12, 13, 15, 16 | pies, ser, zima, ptak | sklep, stary, torba | dom, kot, okno |
| 4 | 17, 19, 18, 20, 21 | dom, kot, stary | ptak, pies, torba | okno, ser, zima, sklep |
| 5 | 22, 23, 24, 25, 26, 27 | ser, ptak, torba, zima | okno, pies, sklep | dom, kot, stary |
| 6 | 28, 29, 30, 32, 33 | okno, pies, sklep | dom, kot, zima, stary | ser, ptak, torba |

## Why this design was used

This design ensured that:
- all participants studied the same vocabulary set
- each word appeared under different conditions across the overall study
- the effect of a condition could be compared more fairly across words

The counterbalanced structure was important because it reduced the chance that one condition would look better simply because it happened to contain easier words.
