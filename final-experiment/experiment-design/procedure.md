# Study Procedure

## Overview

The final study evaluated whether AI-generated mnemonic support could help learners remember beginner Polish vocabulary.

The experiment used 10 Polish words:

- dom
- kot
- okno
- pies
- ser
- zima
- sklep
- stary
- torba
- ptak

Each participant studied the same 10 words, but the format in which each word was shown depended on the participant's assigned group.

## Conditions

The study compared three learning conditions:

### 1. Image + explanation
The participant saw:
- the Polish word
- the English meaning
- the mnemonic explanation
- the AI-generated mnemonic image

### 2. Explanation only
The participant saw:
- the Polish word
- the English meaning
- the mnemonic explanation

### 3. Word only
The participant saw:
- the Polish word
- the English meaning only

## Study flow

The final study procedure was as follows:

1. Each participant received an anonymous participant ID.
2. The participant reviewed the learning materials for their assigned survey group.
3. The participant completed an immediate multiple-choice Polish-to-English quiz.
4. Approximately two weeks later, the same participant completed a delayed quiz using the same vocabulary items.
5. After the delayed quiz, the participant completed feedback questions about the usefulness and clarity of the materials.

## Design rationale

The study used a six-group counterbalanced design so that no single word was permanently tied to one condition. Across the full participant pool, each word appeared under each condition multiple times.

This made it possible to compare:
- baseline word-translation exposure
- verbal mnemonic support
- verbal plus visual mnemonic support

while reducing the chance that the results were driven only by word difficulty.

## Notes

The study was conducted remotely using online forms. Participants were instructed not to spend excessive time studying the materials and not to review the vocabulary again between the immediate and delayed quizzes.

The delayed quiz was the more informative part of the study because immediate performance was high across all conditions.
