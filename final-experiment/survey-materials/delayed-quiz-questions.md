# Delayed Quiz Materials

## Purpose

The delayed quiz measured retention approximately two weeks after the initial learning phase.

This was the more informative test in the project because it showed how well the vocabulary had been retained over time rather than just remembered immediately after study.

## Form structure

The second form contained:

1. participant ID entry
2. delayed multiple-choice vocabulary test
3. feedback questions
4. optional free-text comments

## Participant ID

Participants entered the same anonymous participant ID used in the immediate quiz so that the two forms could be matched.

## Delayed quiz task

Participants completed the same 10-word Polish-to-English vocabulary test again.

The tested words were:

- dom
- kot
- okno
- pies
- ser
- zima
- sklep
- stary
- torba
- ptak

Each item asked the participant to identify the correct English meaning of the Polish word.

## Feedback statements

After the delayed quiz, participants responded to feedback statements using a Likert-style scale.

The key statements included:

1. **The pictures helped me remember the vocabulary better than just the words or descriptions alone.**
2. **The descriptions helped me remember the vocabulary better than just the words alone.**
3. **The images were strongly correlated to the actual meaning of the words.**
4. **The images were not misleading or confusing.**

## Optional comments

The delayed form also included an optional free-text response section so that participants could comment on what they found helpful, confusing, or memorable.

## Notes

The delayed quiz and feedback stage helped identify not only which condition performed best overall, but also which specific images were strong and which ones were misleading or weak.
