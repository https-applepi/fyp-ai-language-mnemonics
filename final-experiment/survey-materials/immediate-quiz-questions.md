# Immediate Quiz Materials

## Purpose

The immediate quiz measured short-term recall immediately after the participant reviewed the assigned study materials.

## Form structure

The first form contained:

1. participant ID entry
2. background information
3. learning phase
4. immediate multiple-choice vocabulary test

## Participant ID

Participants entered their anonymous participant ID so that Quiz 1 and Quiz 2 could be matched without storing names.

## Background information

The first form collected basic background information, including prior familiarity with Polish.

This was used to confirm that participants had little to no Polish background.

## Learning phase

Before the quiz, participants reviewed the 10 Polish vocabulary items in the version assigned to their group.

Depending on the group, each word appeared in one of three formats:

- image + mnemonic explanation
- explanation only
- word only

## Immediate quiz task

After reviewing the materials, participants completed a multiple-choice Polish-to-English test covering the same 10 words.

The tested words were:

- dom
- kot
- okno
- pies
- ser
- zima
- sklep
- stary
- torba
- ptak

Each item asked the participant to identify the correct English meaning of the Polish word.

## Notes

The immediate quiz was designed to test initial encoding of the vocabulary after a brief review of the study materials.

Because many participants performed very well on this quiz, the immediate stage showed a ceiling effect and was less informative than the delayed quiz for distinguishing the learning conditions.
