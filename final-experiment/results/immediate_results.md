# Immediate Results

## Overview
The immediate test measured how well participants could recall the Polish vocabulary shortly after reviewing the learning materials.

## Participants
The first survey had 30 respondents.

## Conditions
The experiment compared three learning conditions:
1. image + mnemonic explanation
2. explanation only
3. word-translation only

The study used a six-group counterbalanced design so that each word appeared equally often in each condition across the overall experiment.

## Main observations
The immediate results were generally strong, with many participants performing very well:
- 23 participants scored full marks
- only a minority of participants made errors on the immediate test

This suggests that the task may have had a ceiling effect in the short term, meaning that immediate recall was high across conditions.

## Condition-level pattern
Even with the strong overall performance, the error breakdown suggested that the image-supported condition was promising.

An early summary of the immediate results showed:
- image + explanation: 3 errors
- explanation only: 5 errors
- word only: 6 errors

This pattern suggests that image-supported mnemonic learning may have offered some advantage, although the differences were not large enough on their own to justify a very strong claim.

## Interpretation
The immediate results suggest that all three conditions supported short-term recall reasonably well, but the image + explanation condition appeared to perform best in the early summary. Because immediate performance was already very high, the short-term test was not the strongest part of the experiment for detecting differences between learning conditions.
