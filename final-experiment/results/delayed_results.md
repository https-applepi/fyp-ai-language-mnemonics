# Delayed Results

## Overview
The delayed test was conducted after a time gap in order to assess retention rather than just immediate short-term recall.

## Why the delayed test mattered
The delayed test was important because the immediate results were very strong overall, making it difficult to distinguish clearly between the learning conditions. Delayed recall is therefore a better measure of whether the mnemonic supports remained memorable over time.

## Main interpretation
The delayed results were more informative than the immediate results. They suggested that the image + explanation condition remained promising, but they also showed that not every generated image was equally effective.

This was an important project finding:
- some AI-generated mnemonic images appeared to support retention
- some images were weak or misleading
- the quality of the mnemonic mattered as much as the fact that an image was present

## Qualitative value of the delayed results
The delayed stage helped reveal which mnemonic designs were genuinely memorable and which ones failed. This supported one of the main conclusions of the project: AI can help generate language-learning materials, but the outputs must be filtered using clear acceptance criteria.

## Interpretation
The delayed findings were therefore useful not only as a measure of retention, but also as evidence that mnemonic quality control is necessary. The project’s contribution was not just testing whether images help, but also identifying what makes an AI-generated mnemonic image educationally useful or weak.
