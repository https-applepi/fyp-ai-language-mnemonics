# Mnemonic Image Quality Rubric

This rubric was used to evaluate whether an AI-generated mnemonic image was suitable for beginner vocabulary learning.

The goal of the image was not simply to be memorable, but to support accurate recall of both the **meaning** and the **pronunciation** of the target Polish word. An image was therefore judged not only on creativity, but also on clarity, correctness, and educational usefulness.

## Practical Criteria for Evaluating AI-Generated Mnemonic Images

| Criterion | Accept when... | Reject when... |
|---|---|---|
| **Meaning salience** | The target meaning is visually dominant and easy to infer. | The cue object overshadows the actual meaning or points more naturally to another answer. |
| **Sound-link clarity** | The phonological cue is recognisable and helps recall pronunciation. | The cue is too weak, too obscure, or too dominant relative to meaning. |
| **Single-scene integration** | Cue and meaning appear in one coherent scene. | The image feels like two unrelated objects placed together without a clear link. |
| **Low visual clutter** | The scene is simple, focused, and easy to parse quickly. | Irrelevant detail or decorative clutter increases confusion. |
| **Lexical correctness** | The target word is spelled correctly and consistently. | The word text is missing, incorrect, or replaced by the wrong language form. |
| **Beginner and global accessibility** | The scene is easy for a novice learner to interpret. | The image depends on niche cultural knowledge or overly abstract symbolism. |

## How this rubric was used

These criteria were used to distinguish stronger mnemonic images from weaker ones during evaluation. In particular, the rubric helped identify cases where an AI-generated image was visually striking but educationally weak.

A strong image needed to do more than include a sound-based cue. It also needed to make the true meaning clear, present the cue and meaning in one coherent scene, avoid clutter, and remain easy for a beginner learner to understand.

## Interpretation

This rubric reflects one of the main conclusions of the project: AI-generated mnemonic images can be useful for language learning, but only when they are filtered using clear quality criteria. Without this filtering step, generated outputs may become confusing, misleading, or less memorable for the correct reason.
